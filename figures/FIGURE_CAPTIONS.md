# Figure Captions

Reference sheet for the seven figures in Fairchild, R. *Engineered Misunderstanding under Psychological Warfare: A Bayesian Signaling Game of Felt-Understanding Collapse in the German Atomausstieg.*

## Figure 1. Phase Diagram of Proposition 1 (Analytical Model)

`Figure_1_Analytical_Phase_Diagram.png`

The threshold curve π*(p) = 1 − c_R / (u_R · p) separates parameter combinations sustaining the separating equilibrium (lower-right region) from those in which only the pooling equilibrium survives (upper-left region). The Atomausstieg calibration (p = 0.5, c_R = 0.35, u_R = 1.0) yields π* = 0.30, marked at the labeled point. The dead zone at low p, where π* = 0 because c_R / u_R ≥ p, indicates populations in which pooling obtains even without adversarial perturbation.

**Location in manuscript:** Section 3.5

---

## Figure 2. Cross-Group Recognition Behavior Under Three Perturbation Regimes

`Figure_2_Recognition_Trajectories.png`

Each curve shows the smoothed mean action rate within the indicated group, averaged across three random seeds. Higher π produces lower sustained recognition behavior; the ordering is monotone and consistent across both groups. The system equilibrates from the high-initial-belief condition within approximately 100 time steps; the steady-state ordering reflects the Analytical Model's comparative-statics prediction.

**Location in manuscript:** Section 5.2

---

## Figure 3. Long-Run Action Rate Across the (π, h) Parameter Space

`Figure_3_Phase_Geometry_pi_homophily.png`

Blue cells indicate parameter combinations sustaining recognition behavior; red cells indicate collapse. The vertical white dashed line marks the analytical threshold π* = 0.30 from the Analytical Model. High homophily (h ≥ 0.85) protects cooperation below the threshold but provides no rescue above it; once π crosses the threshold, no parameter combination tested produces a cooperative basin.

**Location in manuscript:** Section 5.3

---

## Figure 4. Intervention Effectiveness Across the Perturbation Range

`Figure_4_Intervention_Effectiveness.png`

Bridge seeding (placement of recognition-capable agents at structurally central cross-group edges) is the most effective intervention at every perturbation level tested. Cost reduction ranks second; channel hardening provides only modest improvement over the baseline. The vertical dashed line marks π* = 0.30.

**Location in manuscript:** Section 5.4

---

## Figure 5. Adversary Targeting Policies on a Small Stochastic Block Model Network (N = 300)

`Figure_5_Targeting_Policies_SBM.png`

At fixed baseline perturbation π = 0.15, the injection rate γ is varied across the three targeting policies under an exact-count injection budget. The three policies produce statistically similar levels of damage across the injection range, with uniform targeting marginally most damaging at most injection rates but central and bridge targeting occasionally crossing uniform within run-to-run variation. The predicted strong advantage of strategically targeted disinformation is not observed.

**Location in manuscript:** Section 5.5

---

## Figure 5R. Adversary Targeting Policies on a Large Scale-Free Network (N = 5000)

`Figure_5R_Targeting_ScaleFree.png`

Barabási–Albert generator, mean degree 8, with type assignments randomly permuted relative to node identifiers to prevent degree–type correlation. At fixed baseline perturbation π = 0.15, the injection rate γ is varied across the three targeting policies under an exact-count injection budget. Uniform targeting is consistently marginally more damaging than central or bridge targeting, but the gap is small (approximately 0.001 to 0.003 in action rate) and central and bridge are statistically indistinguishable from one another. Baseline action rates are low (approximately 0.025 to 0.032), reflecting the difficulty of sustaining cross-group cooperation in a large heterogeneous scale-free topology even at low perturbation.

**Location in manuscript:** Section 5.5 (robustness check)

---

## Figure 6. Bridge-Seeding Dose-Response Across Perturbation Regimes

`Figure_6_Bridge_Seeding_Dose_Response.png`

The response is approximately linear in dose across all three regimes. Crucially, bridge seeding rescues cooperation even above the analytical threshold: at π = 0.35 (above π* = 0.30), a 30 percent dose lifts the action rate from a baseline of approximately 0.18 to approximately 0.38, more than doubling observable felt understanding.

**Location in manuscript:** Section 5.6
